The three triqui languages triq1251 are spoken in Chicahuaxtla, San Martín Itunyoso and San Juan Copala, communities located in close proximity to the mixtec and amuzgo regions of northwest Oaxaca. Triqui is invariably classified as a Mixtecan language and it is easy to recognize an ample series of cognates across Triqui, Mixtec, and Cuicatec. Two Triqui languages, Chicahuaxtla and Itunyoso, are notable for contrasting lenis and fortis consonants, the latter of long duration and often considered geminates. All three Triqui languages, however, have a complex tonal system, with 8 to 10, or possibly more, tones.
