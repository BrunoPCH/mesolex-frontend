Zongozotla Totonac

The totonac variant spoken in Zongozotla is classified as Highland Totonac high1243, a branch of Sierra Totonacan that includes a significant number of communities in northeastern Puebla (e.g., Huehuetla, Olintla, Coyutla, Coatepec, Ozelonacaztla, Zapotit√°n de Mendoza). These villages are located in an area that stretches about 15 km from Zongozotla in the south to Olintla and Huehuetla in the north. As is general in Sierra Totonacan, Zongozotla unambiguously distinguishes singular and plural in combinations of 1st- and 2nd-person subjects and objects. It has a plural morpheme, qu:-, that indistinctly marks both subjects and objects in transitive verbs. Sierra Totonac also marks the imperfective with a word-final -y, absent in other Totonacan languages. Other markers of Sierra Totonacan are lexical, though many of these words are shared with lowland Papantla Totonac.
